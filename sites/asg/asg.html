Spell Checker Protoype

This is the final assignment for the course "Interface / Interaction Design" at HfK.

This spell checker is trying to help the user to get better at their orthogrophy.

It is possible to write in this space, but the spell check features here are manually added as a prototype and won't appear automatically while typing. Due to some limitations, not all features might work in the live version. For some reason while reloading the page, the navigation might dissappear. In this case please open the page in a new tab or window. To see one usecase and for more information on all the individual features please click on the help button at the right top corner.

Based on my research, the spell checker has incorporated sound and visuals to help the user get a better feel for the correct spelling. Studies and blog posts suggest that hearing the pronuncation of a word can aid with the correct spelling. Therefore each marked word offers a sound option. Personally I noticed that for many words I do not know they'reir right pronunciation, which affected my writing as well. Another method I found to help me was the chunking method where the word is broken down in smaller parts. I noticed for myself that I could remember the correct spelling better after seeing the parts clearly. Often times while reading I rather skim over words without having seen all the letters. Therefore the chunking method encouraged me to look closely at the word without being overwhelmed.

While one doesn't have to feel embarrassed for using the autocorrecting function to much, I feel that the relation between the mistake and correction can be improved. Therefore I tried to put the correct spelling in closer proximity to the word and also show which part of the word was actually causing the error. For the overview on the side, the words are deliberatly written in their corrected version already so that the error is not highlighted too much.
